Organ Transplant Laws Made Easy

Understand the legal framework that governs organ donation and transplantation in India

Overview

The Transplantation of Human Organs and Tissues Act, 1994, is the key legal framework that governs organ donation and transplantation in India. It aims to regulate the removal, storage, and transplantation of human organs and prevent commercial dealings in human organs.

Key Provisions of the Act

Authorization: Organ donation requires written consent from the donor while alive, or from near relatives after death.
Definition of Brain Death: Recognizes brain stem death as a form of death, making deceased organ donation possible.
Prohibition of Commercial Trade: Any buying or selling of organs is strictly prohibited and punishable.
Authorization Committees: Approval is mandatory for unrelated donations, regulated by a government-appointed committee.

Amendments & Recent Developments

  • 2011 Amendment: Included tissues along with organs and streamlined rules for donation and transplantation.
  • 2023 Update: Removal of the upper age limit (65 years) for organ donation in India.

Who Can Donate?

Anyone can pledge to donate their organs after death. Living donors can donate specific organs like one kidney or a portion of the liver to a close relative. In case of brain death, a person can donate multiple organs to save lives.

Types of Donors

  • Living Donors: Donate organs while alive, usually to a family member.
  • Deceased Donors: Donate organs after brain death or cardiac death.
  • Pledged Donors: Individuals who register to donate after death.

Legal Process of Organ Transplantation

  • Step 1: Consent must be provided in writing by the donor or relatives.
  • Step 2: For unrelated donors, an Authorization Committee must approve the transplant.
  • Step 3: Medical fitness and compatibility tests are conducted before transplantation.
  • Step 4: A transplant surgeon performs the procedure in a registered hospital.
  • Step 5: All documentation must be maintained for transparency and legal compliance.

Important Authorities

  • NOTTO: Central government body managing national-level organ donation.
  • ROTTO: Regional Organ & Tissue Transplant Organization.
  • SOTTO: State-level body managing organ transplant coordination.

Penalties Under the Act

  • Imprisonment: Up to 10 years for commercial dealings.
  • Fines: Up to Rs. 20 lakhs depending on the offense.
  • Suspension: Hospitals and doctors may lose licenses for violations.

FAQs

  • Can I donate organs after 65? Yes, age is no longer a restriction if the organs are healthy.
  • Will my body be disfigured? No, organ retrieval is done respectfully and does not affect funeral rituals.
  • Is there any cost to the donor’s family? No, organ donation is completely voluntary and free of charge.
